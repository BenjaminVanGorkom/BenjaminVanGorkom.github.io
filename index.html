Mr. Chen's Computer Science Classrom

“Chen’s comp sci class” refers to the computer science class at Mariposa County High School taught by Mighty Chen , a computer science teacher. For the entirety of the class’s existence, approximately 2 years (from 2022-2022), not only Chen but also the students that have taken the class at Mariposa County High School have gained world fame and renowned. This is primarily attributed to the energetic force and/or aura that the individuals involved exert. Chen himself and students of him have remained incredible illusive, and are almost entirely hidden from the public eye; pictured right is an artist's intrepretation of what an average Chen student may look like based on local folklore and mythology. --->

History

There has only been one other instance of a computer science class taught by Chen, which occurred during the 2020 to 2021 school year. This class, contrary to that of the 2021 to 2022 school year, was an AP (Advanced Placement) class. The 2020 to 2021 class can be described best by testimonials from students that were released to the public by Chen:

  • “Despite my frequent dozing, it was pretty [understandable]. The class managed to get the concepts through in a way that wasn't inherently ulcer-inducing, which is a feat for an AP class” - Blaze Witt
  • “Pretty poggers.” - Sayer Harry
  • “There are too many boys in this class—between this year [2021-2022] and last year [2020-2021], we have seen a 100% increase in women” - Mighty Chen
  • “Continuously beat me in chess. Was mostly poggers [...] Would lose again 10/10.” - Dylan Heard

Culture

Although Chen and his classroom have remained hidden, a vibrant culture has been discovered to have a correlation or connection with the mysterious class. This culture is mainly shared by the “Followers of Chen”, a cultic following based primarily at Mariposa County High School. Many of their motives and origins are unknown, besides the public demonstrations that are oftentimes orchestrated on the Mariposa County High School Campus. Most of these demonstrations consist of complicated and chaotic song and dance and the general pestering of other students and faculty.
